Customer Churn Analysis

Introduction: Customer churn is a critical issue for businesses, and understanding the factors that contribute to churn can help organizations take proactive steps to retain their customers. In this project, a Power BI dashboard was created to analyze customer churn in a virtual internship by Forage. Methodology: The data was analyzed using Power BI, and a dashboard was created to visualize the findings. The dashboard includes various charts and graphs to illustrate the factors that contribute to customer churn, such as the number of tickets, demographic information, and risk assessment. Key Findings: Out of the 7,043 customers analyzed, 1,869 were identified as being at risk of churn. This suggests that there is a need for proactive measures to retain these customers. The number of technical tickets was 2,173, indicating that technical issues may be a significant factor in customer churn. The number of administrative tickets was 885, suggesting that administrative issues may also contribute to churn. 25% of the customers identified as being at risk of churn were senior citizens, 36% had partners, and 17% had dependents. The male to female ratio was almost equal, with males comprising 49.8% of the at-risk group and females comprising 50.2%. Conclusion: The Power BI dashboard provides valuable insights into the factors that contribute to customer churn. By identifying the customers at risk of churn and analyzing the factors that contribute to churn, businesses can take proactive steps to retain their customers. The dashboard presents the findings in an easy-to-understand and visually compelling way, enabling businesses to make informed decisions about how to optimize their customer retention strategies.

Diversity & Inclusions Analysis

Intoduction As part of my role as a Power BI Analyst in a virtual internship with PwC through Forage, I was tasked with analyzing the trends of hires, resigns, promotions, and performance in the company. To achieve this, I cleaned the data for better results and analyzed it on Power BI Desktop. The cover page of the report contains navigation buttons for easy switching between the reports. The insights from the report are as follows: Overview - The company had a total of 500 employees, out of which 434 employees were from the start. In FY20, 66 new candidates were hired, and 47 employees left the company. Currently, there are 453 employees working with the company. The gender balance of the employees in the company is 59% males and 41% females. Hires - A total of 66 new hires were made, with 32 males and 34 females. The average age of new hires was 32 years. Most hires were in the Operations department, followed by Sales & Marketing and Internal Services. The majority of new candidates were recruited for the position of Junior Officer. 32 candidates in the age group of 20-29 were hired. Resigns - 47 employees left the company, with 26 males and 21 females. The majority of employees who left the company were in the age group of 40-49. The average performance rating of resignees was 2.73/4. The majority of employees who left the company had a tenure of 9 years. Promotions - 36 employees were promoted in FY20, and 51 employees were promoted in FY21. Most promotions were in the Operations department for Junior Officers.
